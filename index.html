Erin Maier, astronomer

space telescopes | high contrast imaging | education | outreach

The skyline is a promise, not a bound.

— JOHN MASEFIELD

I am a Ph.D. researcher at the University of Arizona, where I work with Dr. Ewan Douglas in the University of Arizona Space Astrophysics Laboratory. My primary research interests are instrumentation for high-resolution, high contrast imaging, with a scientific focus on dust/debris disks.

My dissertation work focuses on the development of a high-contrast vacuum testbed for the Coronagraphic Debris and Exoplanet Exploring Payload (CDEEP), a proposed small-satellite mission to study debris disks of nearby stars in scattered light. I also work on debris disk studies using Hubble Space Telescope (HST) data and Wide Field Infrared Space Telescope (WFIRST) simulations.

Finally, I am nonbinary, and I use they/them pronouns. Explore the site to learn more about me and my research.
